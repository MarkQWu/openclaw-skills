# Dialogue Platform Rules — Overseas Vertical Short

> Split from `dialogue-craft.md` on 2026-05-15.
> Purpose: platform-native dialogue rules for DramaBox / ReelShort / peer platforms.

## Authority

This file is the first stop for overseas vertical short dialogue polish. It governs line shape before general craft theory and before prestige-TV / long-form exemplars.

Platform dialogue is optimized for:

- first-time mobile viewing,
- burned-in caption legibility,
- short paid-episode retention loops,
- immediate trope recognition,
- explicit emotional and power states,
- rapid comprehension without rewind.

When this file conflicts with McKee / Mamet / Sorkin / prestige-drama examples, this file wins.

## 8 Platform Conventions

- **R1 [MED] — Open on transaction, not atmosphere.** EP1 starts inside the deal, contract, kidnapping, rejection, betrayal, revenge premise, or forced choice.
- **R2 [HIGH for one verbatim anchor] — FL motivation stated in VO within first minute.** Verified example from *Fatal Attraction*: "Once I'm his lover, I'll have the power to avenge my mom!" The platform pattern is overt motive, not prestige subtext.
- **R3 [MED] — ML first appearance = display of power, not dialogue.** Action first. The first spoken line lands after the power differential is already visible.
- **R4 [HIGH] — Trope-forward naming.** Titles and dialogue use direct genre nouns and verbs: "mafia king," "contract," "avenge," "own," "mine," "betrothed." Avoid euphemism when the trope term carries the hook.
- **R5 [HIGH] — Melodrama over subtext.** Platform tone is soapy, melodramatic, and retention-engineered. Prestige-TV restraint is a platform mismatch when it hides the hook.
- **R6 [HIGH] — Caption-legibility drives line writing.** Burned-in captions are baseline. Lines must parse in one glance; SVO beats ambiguous fragments.
- **R7 [MED] — Mafia oath language should feel genre-legible without copying protected expression.** Use original oath / debt / blood / family / territory wording that performs the same platform job. Do not reuse exact phrases, taglines, signature vows, or recognizable line shapes from existing titles.
- **R8 [LOW] — Consigliere / lieutenant role often collapses into phone, henchman, or visual function.** Do not over-invest EP1 words in a named retainer unless the scene makes the role instantly legible.

## Platform-Norm Word Count by Function

Inferred from R1-R6 plus available verbatim anchors.

| Function | Platform word-count range |
|---|---|
| FL text to sister | 2-6 words |
| ML phone ambient business | 4-10 words |
| ML phone closer | 2-4 words |
| FL threat-awareness fragment | 6-14 words with named stake |
| ML observation-as-demand | 2-5 words, usually name-directed rather than prop-directed |
| FL power flip | 5-12 words, usually explicit reveal rather than riddle |
| ML consent-sliding idiom | 2-5 words, usually overt possession such as "You're mine" rather than polite indirection |
| ML orchestration | 4-12 words across 2-3 beats |

## Platform-Native Line Shape

### Preferred

- Direct SVO grammar.
- Named stakes over elegant implication.
- Trope nouns over oblique metaphors.
- Emotional motive stated early when it drives the premise.
- Power verbs: own, choose, avenge, marry, betray, sign, kneel, leave, pay, obey.
- Names used to map hierarchy fast.
- VO used to anchor motive when visual context is insufficient.
- Short lines that are clear, not cryptic.

### Use With Care

- Fragments: valid only when the viewer can decode speaker, object, and stakes instantly.
- Silence: valid only when a single shot carries the beat.
- Politeness-as-menace: usually weaker than overt possession or command.
- Object-as-demand interrogation: usually weaker than interrogating a person or naming the stake.
- Abstract business nouns: replace with concrete platform nouns such as shipment, auction, Russians, shares, debt, contract, blood.

## Risky Prestige-Derived Patterns

These patterns often fail platform norms:

- **Cryptic-fragment-as-threat**: peer platform states stakes plainly.
- **Object-as-demand interrogation**: platform interrogates people, not props.
- **Politeness-as-menace**: platform prefers overt possessive imperative.
- **Abstract nouns in ML business-speak**: platform prefers concrete genre nouns.
- **Cryptic 2-word power flips by FL**: platform FL flips are reveals, not riddles.
- **VO absence**: the largest structural deviation from platform R2 when motive is not otherwise obvious.

## Reconciliation With General Craft

General craft says subtext matters. Platform conditions define how much subtext can survive.

Use this rule:

- If a visual anchor supplies the decoding key inside a 3-second window, compressed subtext may work.
- If no visual anchor supplies the key, use platform-overt trope grammar.
- If a line is elegant but forces the viewer to infer motive, hierarchy, and stake simultaneously, rewrite it.
- If a direct line feels on-the-nose but preserves premise, hook, and emotional state, keep it.

## Platform Dialogue Audit

Run before the general craft checklist in `dialogue-craft.md`.

1. **Function test**: what platform job does the line perform: VO, threat, transaction, reveal, possession, refusal, order, report, consent beat, or power flip?
2. **Glance test**: can the line be understood from burned-in captions in one glance?
3. **Trope test**: does the line name the relevant trope, relationship, or power state directly enough?
4. **Stake test**: does the viewer know what is at risk now?
5. **Motive test**: if this is FL premise setup, is motive overt enough for first-minute comprehension?
6. **Hierarchy test**: does name, title, command, or response shape reveal who has power?
7. **Opacity test**: is any fragment, silence, metaphor, or politeness dependent on prestige-TV patience?
8. **Retention test**: does the line create a reason to keep watching, not merely sound tasteful?
