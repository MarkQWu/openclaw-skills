## 1. Episode format

- **Runtime**: **modal ~90 seconds**, range 60-120s. Rolling Stone (A-tier) explicitly "approximately 90-second increments"; Filmora echoes "only 90 seconds per episode." 2-minute episodes are the outer bound, not the median. **[HIGH]** — Rolling Stone verified by auditor.
- **Aspect ratio**: 9:16 vertical. **[HIGH]**
- **Series length**: 50-100 episodes, 60-80 typical. Do not hard-code "60". **[HIGH]** — Variety, Real Reel, Stage 32 job posts.
- **Word count**: practitioner estimates 40-60K words total across 60-80 episodes (≈600-900 words/ep). **[MED]** — from job-listing-adjacent sources, no platform spec.

**Implication for vertical drama work**: typical demo outlines target "~2-minute episodes / 500-700 words." Platform-mode target is **~90s / ~300-400 words**. ⚠️ Some client briefs specify 137s baselines — that is 50% longer than platform mode. Needs client reconciliation. Writing to 137s means writing against market mode; writing to ~90s means pushing back on client spec.

## 2. Hook window + retention physics

- **3-second drop-off cliff**: 50-60% of viewer churn happens by 3 seconds. **[MED — auditor downgraded from HIGH]** — OpusClip data is for YouTube Shorts generally, not vertical-drama-specific. Plausible by analogy, not directly verified. The "15-second static opening shot" pattern is still clearly against platform grammar by every source consulted.
- **Per-episode beat engine** (Real Reel practitioner playbook): **[LOW — auditor flagged as unverified]**: the Hook/Friction/Spike/Button framework may live only in Real Reel's gated full article; the free teaser does not contain it. Treat as one practitioner's proposal, not industry convention.
  - `0-15s` — Hook (establish conflict, not setup)
  - `15-60s` — Friction (escalate, deepen stake)
  - `60-90s` — Spike (peak reversal or reveal)
  - Last `5-10s` — Button (cliffhanger / next-ep hook)
- **E1 should "detonate," not ease in** — open mid-conflict (kidnapping, rejection, confrontation). Successful TikTok-viral titles (Fated to My Forbidden Alpha, Mafia Lover) open on active conflict, not atmosphere.

**Implication for vertical drama work**: a 15-second motionless close-up in EP1 is not stylistic boldness — it is a direct violation of the dominant retention physic. Must go.

## 3. Paywall mechanics

- **First paywall**: typically at ep 10/11 transition, but **floats between ep 6 and ep 11** depending on title (Real Reel verified by auditor). First 5-10 episodes free. **[MED — audit downgraded from HIGH; variance wider than initial research suggested]**
- **Secondary paywalls at ep 20 and ep 30**: asserted by Real Reel writers' playbook only. **[MED — single strong source]**
- **Pricing per episode**: $0.30-$0.50 commonly cited, but **the "$0.30" may be a Real Reel rhetorical anchor ("worth thirty cents") that SEO sites have laundered into fact**. **[LOW]** — auditor flagged C-tier echo chamber.
- **Full-series unlock — CONFLICT**:
  - SEO-cluster sites (HitPaw, Tenorshare, Wondershare): $8-$15.
  - **Rolling Stone (A-tier): $20-$40 to finish.**
  - **Rolling Stone number is 2-3x higher. Use $20-$40 as the working estimate; the $8-$15 is likely wrong or stale.** **[RED FLAG]**
- **ARPU / conversion rate**: not publicly published. **[UNVERIFIED]**

**Implication**: the "paywalls at 11/20/30" assumption common in imported outlines is **half-verified**. EP11 is canon; EP20 and EP30 are plausible but thin. Plot peaks at those episodes should be preserved; treating them as immovable paywall gates is speculative.

## 4. Genre context

- **Recognized genres on DramaBox/ReelShort**: mafia romance, werewolf/fated mate, billionaire/CEO, revenge, enemies-to-lovers, campus romance. **[HIGH]** — from platform catalogs.
- **Dark academia**: not a first-class genre tag on these platforms. Closest match is campus enemies-to-lovers (e.g., *In Other Words, I Love You* on DramaBox UK).
- **Canonical hits cited**:
  - Werewolf: *Fated to My Forbidden Alpha* (60 ep, Aug 2023, drove 1.5M new users) — Rolling Stone.
  - Billionaire: *Never Divorce a Secret Billionaire Heiress* (55 ep).
  - Mafia catalog (unranked, not top-verified): *Mafia Lover*, *The Mafia's Obsession*, *Mafia Boss Dean Russo*, *Kidnapped by the Mafia King*, *I Went to the Mafia Boss for a Baby* (55 ep).

**Implication for vertical drama work**: when a client specifies mafia × dark academia as the brief, dark academia as an aesthetic layer over mafia romance is a reasonable creative positioning — the platform's primary genre tag would be **mafia romance**, with the Ivy League / classical-studies setting as differentiator within that category. No re-positioning needed; just be aware that "dark academia" itself is not how viewers discover this title on the platform.

## 5. Dialogue / VO conventions

- **VO is standard, not optional**. The "I'm ____, and I feel ____ about ____" self-introduction VO formula is cited from Paste Magazine but auditor's Paste fetch returned 403 — **unverified**. **[LOW]** — single source, not auditor-verified. Treat "VO is normal" as directionally right; the specific formula claim is shakier.
- **Burned-in captions are baseline production**, not user-toggled. Write dialogue that reads as well in caption form as spoken.
- **Line length**: no platform style guide is publicly available. Practitioner-side guidance from Real Reel / Final Draft suggests short lines, but no documented word cap.
- **Tone**: "soapy, melodramatic, reverse-engineered from what sells" (John August). Trope-forward, not detail-rich.

**Implication for vertical drama work**: a prestige-TV minimalist dialogue aesthetic (terse two-word lines like "Hospital or gone." or layered observations like "Three years, and her hand doesn't shake.") is a platform mismatch — too clever, too restrained. Platform-native lines are more direct and emotionally obvious.

## 6. Beat structure

- **Gwen Hayes 4-Phase 20-Beat is NOT industry convention** for vertical drama. It's a **romance-novel coaching frame** that has been imported by analogy. No platform / analyst / practitioner source for vertical drama uses it. **[HIGH — confirmed by research absence]**
- **What successful titles actually do** is not formally published. Beat structure is reverse-engineered from sales data (per John August). Macro-arc conventions are trope-based: meet-cute / claim / crisis / grovel / happy-ever-after.
- **Real Reel's per-episode engine** (Hook / Friction / Spike / Button) is the closest to a vertical-drama-specific framework.

**Implication for vertical drama work**: the 4-Phase / 20-Beat architecture commonly imported into outlines is **framework-imported-by-analogy**, not platform-native. Retain the plot content; discard the structural vocabulary when writing to platform.

## 7. Writer economics (context, not constraint)

- Writer pay: $3K-$6K per project; $8K + 1% profit share for senior work. **[MED]** — Stage 32 listings, Jenova.
- ReelShort uses up to 20 in-house writers. Web-novel author bonuses: $100 sign / $250 completion. **[HIGH]** — Rolling Stone.
- Lead actor day rate $300-$500; 5-7 day shoots, mostly nonunion. **[HIGH]** — Rolling Stone.

## 8. 9:16 physics (writing constraints)

- Default shot: close-up to medium close-up (face + hands + one prop).
- Maximum actors per frame: 2 in dialogue, 3 in crowd.
- Single-subject action — one person does, the other reacts. No simultaneous multi-character blocking.
- No wide establishing shots; replace with INSERT detail (gargoyle / crest / gate / ring / prop).
- No camera directions (PAN / ZOOM / DOLLY) — master scene script, not shooting script.
- Max wordless stretch: ~15s. After 15s, dialogue / VO / new character must enter.

---

## 9. Episode file conventions (standard)

- Standard Hollywood master-scene format. Machine-executable spec: `scripts/format-deliverable.py`.
- Start with `COLD OPEN` then `FADE IN:` — no recap, no pre-roll.
- End with `SMASH CUT TO BLACK.` or `CUT TO BLACK.`
- `(SFX: xxx)` and `(MUSIC: xxx)` cues are suggestions for sound designer, tied to visible action.
