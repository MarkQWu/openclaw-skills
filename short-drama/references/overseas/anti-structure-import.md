# Anti-Structure Import

Purpose: block feature-film and domestic-structure vocabulary from becoming the governing story system for overseas vertical drama.

## Prohibited Terminology

The following terms trigger Hollywood feature-film structure priors in LLMs. Their use causes drift away from overseas vertical-drama platform grammar. Grep for these before finalizing any structural document.

### Hard Banned

| Chinese | English |
|---|---|
| 三幕 / 三幕剧 | three-act / three act |
| 第一幕 / 第二幕 / 第三幕 | act one / act two / act three / Act I / Act II / Act III |
| 开端-发展-高潮 | setup-confrontation-resolution |
| 铺垫-冲突-结局 | same structure, Chinese variant |

### Alert-Level, Not Banned

Save the Cat family terms are Hollywood feature-film variants:

- `catalyst`
- `break into 2`
- `midpoint`
- `all is lost`
- `dark night of the soul`
- `break into 3`
- `finale`
- `B story`

Before using any alert-level term, confirm the term names a concrete platform function, such as hook, paid-episode pressure, relationship choice, reveal, reversal, or cliffhanger. If it only imports feature-film vocabulary, treat it as banned.

## Correct Alternatives

Use viewer-facing functions and target-market pressure labels:

- hook
- opening pressure
- paid-episode pressure
- relationship choice
- reveal
- reversal
- cliffhanger
- agency choice
- moral turning point
- platform-readable visual anchor

Gwen Hayes / romance-beat vocabulary may be used as private scaffold, but it is not platform-native authority and must not appear as the final structural surface.

## Review Rule

When a structure term appears, ask:

1. Does it name a vertical-platform function?
2. Does it control a paid pressure, reveal/reversal, relationship turn, or cliffhanger?
3. Would the same term push the model toward feature-film pacing?

If the answer to 1 or 2 is no, or the answer to 3 is yes, replace the term with the platform function it is supposed to perform.
